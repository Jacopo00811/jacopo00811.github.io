San Francisco Crime: Two Decades of Data

This portfolio explores interactive temporal and geographic patterns in San Francisco crime, with attention to how offense timing changes across day and night and across districts. It also highlights how reporting-driven dynamics and policing focus can create feedback loops that shape what the data appears to show.

Pairwise Temporal Pattern Similarity

Open Image

Embezzlement and Fraud show the most similar temporal behavior while Fraud and Prostitution show the most dissimilar day-vs-night reporting pattern.

The Embezzlement vs Fraud pairing represents two report-driven crimes where added investigation into Fraud can naturally uncover more Embezzlement, illustrating a feedback loop in observed counts.

Interactive Hourly Focus Crimes

Open Interactive View

This interactive chart lets you click legend categories to compare crime-type activity across the 24-hour cycle without clutter.

Robbery, Prostitution, and Burglary peak at night, while Embezzlement, Forgery and Counterfeiting, Fraud, and Gambling peak in work hours.

Method and Feedback-Loop Note

If police redeploy officers using these hotspot outputs, reported incidents in targeted areas can increase because data collection intensifies where police look, linking directly to the dirty-data feedback-loop risk and potentially reducing attention elsewhere.
